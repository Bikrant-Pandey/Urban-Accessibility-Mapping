# Urban-Accessibility-Mapping
This project analyzes how easily people in Kathmandu District can access major roads and hospitals using QGIS. Road and hospital data from OpenStreetMap were processed and clipped to the district boundary, and buffer zones were created to represent accessible areas. These layers were combined to identify well-connected regions and areas that lack both transport and healthcare access. The results show that central areas are better served, while outer parts of the district have lower accessibility, highlighting where improvements in infrastructure and services are needed.
